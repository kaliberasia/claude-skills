## Structural Patterns

### Save Strong Metaphors for Re-hooks

**Pattern:** Don't spend best metaphors in the intro. Plant seeds early, develop fully later.

**Source:** AI Transformation article (2025-11-28)

**Example:**
- V1: Hockey stick metaphor in intro (spent early, no payoff)
- Edited: Hockey stick removed from intro, deployed in Section 5 with full development ("Deploy ChatGPT, give everyone access..." fantasy + boomerang resolution)

**Validation count:** 1/3

---

### Close Mirrors Intro Length

**Pattern:** Proportional framing - if intro is 3 paragraphs, close should be ~3 paragraphs. Creates structural balance.

**Source:** AI Transformation article (2025-11-28)

**Example:**
- V1: 4 short sentences as close
- Edited: 3 full paragraphs matching intro weight

**Validation count:** 1/3

---

### Categorize When Critiquing

**Pattern:** When criticizing a category of things, break into distinct sub-categories rather than one blob.

**Source:** AI Transformation article (2025-11-28)

**Example:**
- V1: "Most AI tools are foundational model wrappers" (one category)
- Edited: Two distinct categories:
  1. Foundational model wrappers (thin UI on top of GPT/Claude)
  2. N8N/Make/Zapier (flowcharts with AI magic dust)

**Validation count:** 1/3

## Content Patterns

### Name Concepts

**Pattern:** Create memorable labels for ideas. Concepts stick better than descriptions.

**Source:** AI Transformation article (2025-11-28)

**Examples:**
- "AI slop" (shallow outputs that create more problems than they solve)
- "Business^AI" (amplification works both ways)

**Validation count:** 1/3

---

### Name the Fantasy

**Pattern:** Explicitly articulate what the reader secretly hopes for. Makes them recognize their own thinking.

**Source:** AI Transformation article (2025-11-28)

**Example:**
- "Deploy ChatGPT, give everyone access, and suddenly the team is twice as effective. That expectation is setting their businesses up for failure."

**Validation count:** 1/3

---

### Name Specific Failure Behaviors

**Pattern:** Don't just say "most fail" - describe exactly what failing looks like.

**Source:** AI Transformation article (2025-11-28)

**Example:**
- Generic: "Most will chase tools. Most will give up."
- Specific: "When they don't see the hockey stick, they'll wrap up the initiative and call it a failure. Or they'll chase the next shiny tool that promises the shortcut they're looking for."

**Validation count:** 1/3

---

### Provide Alternatives When Critiquing

**Pattern:** When saying something is bad, show what's better. Don't leave reader stuck.

**Source:** AI Transformation article (2025-11-28)

**Example:**
- Critique: "Foundational model wrappers are a waste of time"
- Alternative: "Leaders would be better off learning to use the foundational models directly. Done right, they're more effective than any wrapper."

**Validation count:** 1/3
