# Interview Guide

Structured question sequences for extracting knowledge during the ghostwriting interview phase. Adapt depth based on format.

---

## Format-Specific Interview Depth

| Format | Interview Length | Question Depth |
|--------|-----------------|----------------|
| Long-form (600-800 words) | 8-12 questions across all phases | Full exploration |
| Medium (LinkedIn ~350 words) | 5-7 questions, focus on core insight | Core + one supporting layer |
| Short-form (Chili/X) | 3-4 questions, single insight focus | One sharp idea only |

---

## Phase 1: Topic Grounding

**Purpose:** Understand what the piece is actually about and why it matters.

**Core Questions:**
1. "What's the one thing you want readers to walk away understanding?"
2. "Why does this topic matter to you personally? What experience or observation sparked it?"
3. "Who is this for?" — Offer options:
   - Business leaders (executives, COOs, founders)
   - Senior marketers (CMOs, brand strategists)
   - Professionals interested in AI

   **Why this matters:** Answer determines third-person framing throughout the piece ("leaders" vs "marketers" vs "professionals").

**Depth Questions (Long-form only):**
4. "What's the conventional wisdom on this topic that you disagree with?"
5. "What's at stake if people don't understand this?"

---

## Phase 2: Insight Extraction

**Purpose:** Surface the unique perspective, not just information.

**Core Questions:**
1. "What do you see that others miss about this topic?"
2. "What's the uncomfortable truth here that most people avoid?"
3. "If you could explain this to someone in one sentence, what would it be?"

**Depth Questions (Long-form only):**
4. "What mental model or framework helps you think about this?"
5. "What's the root cause beneath the surface symptoms?"
6. "What historical pattern or analogy illuminates this?"

---

## Phase 3: Evidence & Examples

**Purpose:** Ground abstract insights in concrete reality.

**Core Questions:**
1. "What's a specific example or story that illustrates this?"
2. "What data, observation, or signal supports this view?"

**Depth Questions (Long-form only):**
3. "Can you give me 2-3 signals from different domains that point to this?"
4. "What would a skeptic say, and how would you respond?"
5. "What have you personally experienced that validates this?"

---

## Phase 4: Implications & Action

**Purpose:** Connect insight to reader's life and decisions.

**Core Questions:**
1. "So what? What should someone do with this understanding?"
2. "What changes when someone sees this clearly?"

**Depth Questions (Long-form only):**
3. "What's the first practical step someone could take?"
4. "What does this mean for the reader's decisions, career, or life?"
5. "What becomes possible when someone operates from this insight?"

---

## Interview Execution Guidelines

**Pacing:**
- Ask 1-2 questions at a time, not all at once
- Listen for threads to pull - follow energy, not script
- Let Shawn's natural connections guide the flow

**Quality Signals:**
- Personal stories and specific examples = gold
- Energy/passion in response = pursue further
- Vague or generic answers = probe deeper

**What to capture:**
- Distinctive phrases and language
- Specific numbers, dates, examples
- Emotional hooks and tension points
- Contrarian angles and surprising takes

**Red Flags:**
- "Everyone knows..." = likely surface-level
- No specific examples = needs grounding
- Explaining what, not why = dig for root cause

---

## Format-Specific Adaptations

### Long-form (600-800 words)
- Full interview sequence
- Explore multiple supporting angles
- Gather excess material - can trim in outline phase
- Look for: problem → root cause → reframe → path forward arc

### Medium (LinkedIn ~350 words)
- Focus on single powerful insight + one supporting proof point
- Skip Phase 4 depth questions - action implied in insight
- Look for: hook → insight → implication structure

### Short-form (Chili/X)
- One sharp insight only
- One vivid example or proof point
- Look for: tension/hook + resolution in single thought
- If multiple good insights emerge, save others for separate pieces
